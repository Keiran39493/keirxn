keirxn

A private personal dashboard built around two things: knowing how your body is performing, and eating well. It combines age-adjusted health analysis with a curated recipe collection — all behind a single login.

Page 1

Dashboard

The dashboard is the home page. Three large ring gauges show your most important recovery signals at a glance — Body Battery, Sleep Score, and Stress Level. Below that, a grid of metric cards covers the full picture:

HRV
Heart rate variability in ms — the strongest readiness signal. Compared against your 7-day baseline to flag stress or recovery.
Resting HR
Beats per minute recorded at complete rest. Lower values indicate a stronger, more efficient cardiovascular system.
VO₂ Max
Estimated aerobic capacity in mL/kg/min. One of the highest-ROI metrics for predicting long-term health and longevity.
Body Battery
0–100 energy reserve score, calculated from HRV, stress, and sleep. Refills overnight, drains through activity and stress.
Sleep Score
Overall sleep quality rated 0–100, based on duration, deep sleep, REM, overnight HRV, and resting HR.
Stress Level
Average and peak stress throughout the day, derived from HRV patterns. Elevated chronic stress suppresses recovery and immune function.
Steps
Daily step count tracked against your step goal. Distance in metres also recorded.
SpO₂
Blood oxygen saturation percentage. Average and lowest readings across the day.
Calories
Active calories burned during the day, separate from resting metabolic rate.
Respiration
Average breathing rate in breaths per minute, split between waking and sleep.
Fitness Age
Biological age estimate based on your VO₂ Max. Can be lower than your actual age with good aerobic fitness.

The dashboard also generates automatic text insights — flagging when HRV drops more than 15% below your weekly average, when sleep score falls below 60, when sleep duration is under 6 hours, when body battery hits critical low, or when daily stress exceeds 65/100. Each insight includes a specific action to take that day. The bottom of the page shows 7-day sparkline charts for HRV, Sleep Score, Body Battery, and Stress.

Page 2

Insights

The Insights page puts your numbers in the context of the broader population. Enter your age, gender, height, and weight at the top — the page auto-saves to your profile. BMI is calculated in real time. Then enter your actual metric values and each card scores you against age-matched reference data from published population health studies.

Body Battery

Body Battery has no age-adjusted population data, so it uses a fixed 4-tier colour scale: 75–100 is High (green), 50–74 is Good (blue), 25–49 is Low (orange), and 0–24 is Critically Low (red). Each tier comes with guidance on what activity level is appropriate.

Each metric card also includes a plain-English explanation of what the metric means, a personalised next-tier goal that updates as you type, and a list of evidence-based actions to improve it.

Page 3

Recipes

A private, searchable recipe collection adapted from The Body Coach. Recipes are stored in a CSV and loaded entirely client-side — no database, instant search. Each recipe card shows a photo, dietary tags, prep + cook time, and star rating. Clicking through to a recipe shows the full ingredient list with amounts and a step-by-step method.

Search works across recipe names and ingredients. Recipes are organised by dietary tag:

Technical

How it works

keirxn is a lightweight Flask application served by Gunicorn, deployed on Render, with keirxn.com pointed at it via Namecheap DNS. The frontend is plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no frameworks. Chart.js handles the 7-day sparklines on the dashboard.

Backend Python / Flask / Gunicorn
Frontend Vanilla HTML, CSS, JS
Charts Chart.js v4
Hosting Render (web service)
Domain Namecheap → keirxn.com
Font Inter (Google Fonts)
Auth SHA-256 hash, 8h session

Profile data (name, age, height, weight, units, gender) is persisted in profile.json. Dark mode preference is stored in localStorage with an anti-flash inline script in the <head> to prevent a white flicker before the CSS loads.
