Questions for Interview
Introduction
1. Can you briefly describe the core mission of demenz.li and the main programs you run here in
Liechtenstein?
I) Counseling for people with dementia and their family members; long-term follow-up
counseling. Educational programs for family members of people with dementia (general
information sessions, lectures, technical and administrative information, support groups, courses
to improve understanding of the disease, …), educational initiatives for non-family caregivers
(e.g., family support services, hospice care, inpatient caregivers and family members, municipal
staff, the general public, …), projects to relieve the burden on family members, prevention
projects, projects like this one …; AND
II) “Vergissmeinnicht” day center for people with dementia, including transportation services
2. What does your current landscape look like? Do you rely mostly on private donors, government
grants, or foundation trusts?
I) 2/3 funded by the state; we must cover the remainder ourselves each year (70,000/year)
II) 1/3 funded by the state; we must cover the remainder ourselves (100,000/year; guaranteed
through 2028)
3. How much time and resources does your team currently dedicate to actively searching for and
applying for funding?
10 -15 %

6. In previous interviews, funders mentioned that established relationships often dictate who gets
funded. Have you found it difficult to break into these donor networks?
In 2025, we invested in a fundraiser. We also have our own (newly) established network. But
here’s the thing: if we need CHF 170T a year, some people say that we’re essentially fulfilling a
basic public service and that the government should be funding it. And the government says the
same thing … ;-)
Strategy & Impact Reporting
7. Some Funders noted that local NGOs sometimes struggle to align their strategies with donor
expectations. What are the biggest challenges you face when structuring a funding proposal?
The expected “formats” of the applications vary WIDELY. On top of that, some “discovered”
donors don’t want to be recommended to others or thanked at all. Some would rather just give 
money and then be left in peace. Some staff on the “front lines” don’t even know how to deal
with applicants. Some want a lot of paperwork, others just a single line. So if you need a lot of
money, it’s very tricky.
8. How do you currently track and report the impact of your work at demnz.li?
Figures: Client registrations, quantitative data on interventions. Satisfaction among day visitors.
9. Do you have the capacity to regularly update donors with structured impact reports or success
stories, or is that an administrative burden?
It varies. At the very least, everyone who donates over CHF 1,000 receives the annual report.
We’ve only had experience with major donors this year.
