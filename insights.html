What it means

HRV measures the variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. A higher HRV means your autonomic nervous system is well-balanced — able to switch efficiently between stress and recovery. It is one of the strongest predictors of cardiovascular fitness, resilience to stress, and longevity.

  • Consistent sleep and wake times daily
  • Breathwork or meditation (10 min/day)
  • Cold exposure (brief cold showers)
  • Limit alcohol — it significantly suppresses HRV
  • Avoid high-intensity training when HRV is low

What it means

Resting HR is the number of beats per minute your heart takes at complete rest. A lower resting HR indicates a stronger, more efficient heart that pumps more blood per beat. Elite endurance athletes typically have resting HRs of 40–50 bpm. High resting HR is linked to increased cardiovascular risk.

Enter your resting HR and age to see your personalised target.
How to improve
  • Regular aerobic exercise (run, cycle, swim)
  • Build volume gradually — zone 2 is most effective
  • Maintain a healthy body weight
  • Manage chronic stress (cortisol elevates HR)
  • Limit caffeine, especially later in the day
  • Quality, consistent sleep lowers resting HR over time

What it means

Garmin's Body Battery uses HRV, stress data, and activity to estimate your energy reserves on a 0–100 scale. It accumulates during restful sleep and drains through activity and stress. Think of it as your daily fuel tank — it shows how recovered you are and how much you can push today.

Reading the score
  • 75–100: Fully charged — push hard
  • 50–74: Good — moderate to high activity OK
  • 25–49: Low — light activity only
  • 0–24: Critical — rest is essential
How to improve
  • Prioritise 7–9 hours of uninterrupted sleep
  • Avoid intense training when below 25
  • Short naps (10–20 min) can partially recharge
  • Manage daily stress — it drains battery constantly
  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol before sleep
  • A consistent sleep schedule maximises overnight recharge

What it means

Garmin's sleep score rates overall sleep quality 0–100 based on duration, sleep stages (deep, REM, light), overnight HRV, resting HR, and movement. Deep sleep drives physical repair; REM consolidates memory and emotion. A score above 85 indicates excellent, restorative sleep. Chronic poor sleep accelerates ageing and impairs performance.

How to improve
  • Target 7–9 hours — no exceptions at the weekend
  • Same sleep and wake time every day (including weekends)
  • Keep bedroom cool: 16–19°C (60–67°F)
  • No screens 60 min before bed — use blue-light mode
  • No caffeine after 14:00
  • Avoid alcohol within 3 hours of sleep
  • Wind-down routine: dim lights, journalling, stretching
